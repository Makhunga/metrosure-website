The following four pillars establish why complete separation of Metrosure's business activities is non-negotiable:

### 1. Regulatory Clarity

FSP-regulated activities **must** be clearly distinguished from unregulated consulting services. The Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) requires that consumers can immediately identify when they are engaging with a regulated financial services provider versus an unregulated advisory service.

**Key requirement:** A consumer visiting the website must never be confused about whether they are purchasing a regulated insurance product or engaging consulting services.

### 2. Brand Identity

Each business serves fundamentally different audiences with entirely different needs:

| Aspect | Insurance (Metrosure) | Consulting (Metro Risk) |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **Audience** | Individuals, families, SMEs | Corporate risk managers, executives |

| **Relationship** | Transactional | Partnership |
| **Decision-maker** | Individual/family | Committee/board |
| **Communication style** | Simple, reassuring | Technical, analytical |

Attempting to serve both audiences from a single website creates cognitive dissonance and dilutes both value propositions.

### 3. Operational Independence

The two business lines operate with completely different commercial dynamics:

| Metric | Insurance | Consulting |
|--------|-----------|------------|

### 4. Risk Mitigation

> **âš ï¸ CRITICAL WARNING**

### 1.2 Regulatory Landscape

Metrosure Insurance Solutions operates as a licensed Financial Services Provider under the Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services (FAIS) Act. This regulatory status brings significant obligationsâ€”and significant risks if not properly managed.

#### FSCA Compliance Requirements

The FSCA requires that FSPs:
- Clearly identify themselves as regulated entities in all client communications
- Provide mandated disclosures before product recommendations
- Maintain clear records of advice and transactions
- Ensure marketing materials comply with Treating Customers Fairly (TCF) outcomes

#### The Compliance Risk of Mixed Content

> **âš ï¸ CRITICAL WARNING**
> Mixing FSP-regulated insurance content with unregulated consulting content on a single website creates audit risk and regulatory exposure.

**Specific risks include:**

1. **Audit complexity** â€“ FSCA auditors may struggle to distinguish which content relates to regulated activities, leading to extended audits and potential findings

2. **Disclosure confusion** â€“ Insurance products require specific disclosures that are inappropriate for consulting services; mixed content makes correct disclosure placement difficult

3. **Advice boundary ambiguity** â€“ When does risk consulting become financial advice? A combined website makes this boundary dangerously unclear

4. **TCF compliance** â€“ Treating Customers Fairly outcomes must demonstrably apply to insurance clients; consulting clients have different (unregulated) expectations

#### FSCA Enforcement Reality

The FSCA has demonstrated willingness to take enforcement action against FSPs that fail to maintain proper compliance standards:

- **Administrative penalties** â€“ Fines for compliance failures
- **Licence conditions** â€“ Additional requirements imposed on non-compliant FSPs

## 2. Recommended Website Architecture

### 2.1 Separate Domains

| Business | Domain | Purpose |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Metrosure Insurance | metrosure.co.za | Consumer insurance products and services |
| Metro Risk Consulting | metrorisk.co.za | Corporate risk consulting services |

### 2.2 No Cross-Contamination

The websites should operate as independent entities:

- **No shared navigation** â€“ Users should not be able to accidentally navigate from one business to another
- **Distinct visual identity** â€“ Different colour palettes, typography, and imagery
- **Separate compliance frameworks** â€“ Each site implements only the disclosures relevant to its regulated status
- **Independent analytics** â€“ Separate tracking to understand distinct customer journeys

### 2.3 Appropriate Cross-Referencing

Where legitimate business reasons exist to reference the sister company:

- Use clear, compliant language: *"Metrosure is part of the Metro group, which also includes Metro Risk Consulting for corporate clients"*
- Place references in footer or "About" sections, not primary navigation
- Ensure any links open in new windows/tabs to maintain clear context
- Never imply that consulting services are FSP-regulated
