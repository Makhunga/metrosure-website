## Company Overview

### Brand Name Meaning
- **Metro** = A subway system, rapid transit rail transport system
- **Sure** = Without any doubt, completely confident that one is right
- **Combined meaning:** A company that will grow faster while completely confident they are on the right side of the law

### Tagline/Motto
**"Taking you to the Future"**

### Company Structure

**Metrosure Group** (Umbrella - Founded 2013)
- **Metrosure Consult** - Insurance & advisory services
- **Metrosure Insurance Brokers (Pty) Ltd** - Retail direct insurance, sales & marketing, telemarketing

### Company Identity
- **Full Legal Name:** Metrosure Insurance Brokers (Pty) Ltd
- **Trading Name:** Metrosure Insurance Brokers (Pty) Ltd
- **Registration Number:** 2016/113504/07
- **FSP Number:** 47089 (Authorized Financial Service Provider)
- **Founded:** March 18, 2016 (Metrosure Insurance Brokers) | 2013 (Metrosure Group)
- **FSP Authorization Date:** February 7, 2017
- **Regulator:** Financial Services Conduct Authority (FSCA)

### Ownership & Background
- Owned by previously disadvantaged individuals from KwaZulu-Natal
- Founded with a plan to take the business to a formidable position in the South African market
- Black-owned enterprise committed to meaningful economic participation of previously disadvantaged people
- Focus on stimulating the second economy through youth employment

## Tone of Voice Guidelines

### 2025 B2B Focus - Do:
- Emphasize **results and metrics** ("75% sales increase", "95% QA average")
- Position as **sales & marketing partner**, not just insurance broker
- Highlight **data-driven approach** and customer profiling
- Stress **youth employment** and transformation story
- Use **performance language** ("proven track record", "quality business")
- Reference **specific client success** (TFG/Jet partnership)

### Do:
- Use confident, professional language
- Emphasize speed and reliability (like a metro train)
- Highlight certainty and trust ("sure" in the name)
- Reference transformation and empowerment
- Speak to South African context and values

### Don't:
- Use generic insurance jargon
- Sound corporate and impersonal
- Ignore the company's transformation story
- Use overly complex financial terminology
- Make unsubstantiated claims

### Key Phrases (2025):
- "Data-driven insights"
- "Customer profiling and segmentation"
- "Quality assurance team"
- "Recruit and develop young individuals"
- "Proven track record"
- "75% average sales increase"
- "95% QA average"
- "Consistent, reliable quality business"

### Sample B2B Copy:
- "We don't just sell insurance - we build sales engines"
- "Transform your retail space into a revenue stream"
- "Our QA team ensures 95% compliance on every sale"
- "75% sales increase for retail partners in the first 6 months"
- "We recruit and develop young individuals - they are the future"
